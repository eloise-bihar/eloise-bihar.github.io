Bihar Lab

Soft Bioelectronics & Hydrogel-Based Sensors

We are an interdisciplinary research group in the Department of Materials Design and Innovation at the University at Buffalo. Our work bridges materials science, electrochemistry, and bioengineering to create soft electronic systems using printed and adaptive hydrogels.

Explore the research Join / collaborate

Research Areas

We design materials and devices that communicate with biology using ions, electrons, and light.

Hydrogel-Based Biosensors

Flexible platforms for monitoring metabolites and infection markers in complex environments (wounds, saliva, sweat, neural tissues).

Organic Electrochemical Transistors (OECTs)

Printed OECT architectures for multiplexed sensing, data-rich readout, and integration with AI-driven analytics.

Hydrogel Electrolytes & Ion Transport

Hydrogels with tunable hydration, ionic conductivity, and self-healing behavior for soft electronics and energy devices.

Bioactive & Therapeutic Materials

Electronic patches and implants to actively modulate tissue response.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Eloise Bihar Assistant Professor, MDI

Dr. Bihar’s research focuses on soft bioelectronics, hydrogel-based sensors, organic electrochemical transistors, and multifunctional cryogels for wound healing and neural interfaces.

Before joining UB, she worked on printed electronics and biointerfaces across academia and industry. She enjoys building interdisciplinary teams and translating materials discoveries into impactful devices.
